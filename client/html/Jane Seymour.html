Jane Seymour: c. 1507 - 1536





Childhood: 1507 - 1520s

Birth: c. 1507/8 in Wulfhall Manor, (demolished in 1723) Wiltshire, England.

Parents: Sir John Seymour and Margaret Wentworth (minor nobility).

Prominent Siblings: Older brother: Edward born c. 1500 and younger brother: Thomas born c. 1508/9



Early Life: Not much is known about her early life. Her education was not as expansive as Anne Boleyn's and Katherine of Aragon's, but she was taught to read and write, and was said to enjoy needlework and horseback riding.



Lady-in-Waiting to Katherine of Aragon and Anne Boleyn: 1520s - 1536

1520s (exact date not known): Jane comes to court to serve as a lady-in-waiting to Katherine of Aragon. There she befriends her daughter Mary and sympathizes with her throughout her mother's divorce proceedings.



1531: Katherine is banished from court into exile, and Jane transfers into the service of Anne.

September 4th - 10th, 1535: Henry and Anne, on a progress through England, stop to stay at Wulfhall for six days, hosted by Jane and her parents. (Monarchs going on progress throughout their lands, and stopping to stay at the houses of nobility was quite common at the time.) Henry already knew Jane socially, as she was a lady-in-waiting to both Katherine and Anne, but this is the first time that he met her out of court. It is said that this is when their affair started. When Henry returned to court, their affair continued.

Affair With Henry: September 1535 - May 1536 Jane, seeing how Henry's affair with Anne had started passionately, and then ended in her death as he had grown tired of the way she was acting; had been coached by her family to act the exact opposite towards Henry during their affair, so she would have a chance of supplanting Anne. So Jane acted even tempered, demure, quietly dignified and docile when Henry was courting her, and refused to consummate the affair until she was married. This refusal was the same tactic Anne used, so in this way, any child with the king could be born in wedlock and considered legitimate. Henry would send her expensive presents and even sent her some money. When she received the money from his messenger, she didn't accept it, rather saying that she would rather Henry give her money when she was married, in order to preserve her honor at court. Henry enjoyed hearing this, and from then on, arranged for her brother Edward and his wife Anne Stanhope to have rooms in court next to his where Jane was also to stay. Edward and Anne would then act as chaperones when Henry visited Jane.

January 29th, 1536: Anne Boleyn walks in on Jane sitting on the lap of Henry, and this causes her to miscarries the baby boy she was pregnant with.

May 2nd, 1536 Henry angry at Anne's lack of male heirs and wanting an excuse to be rid of her and marry Jane, has his chief advisor, Thomas Cromwell, arrest Anne and accuses her of adultery with five men (one, her own brother) while married to him. If found guilty, the would be guilty of treason, and the penalty for that was death. (The charges were in future decades proved false.) The five men are also arrested. Anne and the men are brought as prisoners to the Tower of London.

May 12th - 15th, 1536: Anne and the five men are all given show trials with a forgone conclusion and declared guilty.

May 17th, 1536: The five men are beheaded at Tower Green in the Tower of London. On the same day, Anne's marriage is declared null and void, and Elizabeth declared illegitimate and no longer a princess or heir to the throne.

May 19th, 1536: Anne is also beheaded at Tower Green. She is buried at the chapel of St. Peter Ad Vicula in the Tower. Anne is beheaded in the morning, and in the afternoon, Henry has a messenger announce to Parliament that he will be bethrothed to Jane soon.





Marriage to Henry VIII: 1536-7

May 20th, 1536: The day after Anne is beheaded, Henry is bethrothed to Jane in a public ceremony at Hampton Court where her family is most likely present.

May 30th, 1536: Jane and Henry are married and she is proclaimed Queen on June 4th. He decides not to proceed with a coronation, as there is plague in London, and also, because it is thought that he wants to wait until she gives him a son first.



Life as Queen of England: Jane was strict and formal, being a stickler for observance of protocol. The lavish entertainments, gaiety, and extravagance of the queen's household, which had reached their peak during Anne Boleyn's time, were replaced by strict decorum. She banned the French fashions Anne had introduced. Jane's only reported involvement in national affairs, was in 1536, was when she asked for pardons for participants in the Pilgrimage of Grace. Henry is said to have rejected this, reminding her of the fate her predecessor met with should she "meddle with his affairs." She was known to have supported the causes of Katherine of Aragon and her daughter Mary, and be a Catholic. The people of England who had not accepted Henry's more Protestant direction for the church, thought that she could help convince him to turn back his religious reforms. On top of her more relaxed demeanor in direct contrast to Anne's, she was also the first wife that Henry had where his previous wives weren't alive. Thus she was considered by all people of England, whether Protestant or Catholic leaning, to be the first legitimate wife of Henry's since Katherine. Due to all this, she was much loved by her people.

June 8th, 1536: Second Act of Succession - saying that Henry's children with Anne Boleyn and Katherine of Aragon were illegitimate, and any heirs to the throne would come from children he had with Jane Seymour.

June 13th, 1536: Mary, Henry's older daughter, realizing that now that her mother is gone, Charles V is not interested in invading England to defend her right to the throne, finally signs her agreement to the Second Act of Succession, agreeing that her parents marriage was invalid, and she is therefore illegitimate.

July 6th, 1536: Now that Mary had signed the Act, Henry decided to go and see her with Jane. Jane, a supporter of the Catholic cause, and wanting to see Mary reinstated as heir to the throne, had been asking Henry to reconcile with Mary. Henry refused to see her until Mary signed the Act. Once this was done, Jane urged Henry to publicly reconcile with Mary.



July 1536: 10 Articles - the leading bishops of the Church of England adopt these articles that lead the church doctrine in a slightly more Protestant direction.

July 22nd, 1536: Henry's illegitimate son, Henry FitzRoy dies at 17 without children, putting an end to the speculation that Henry VIII could get Parliament to declare him as his heir if he didn't have any legitimate sons.

December 17th, 1536: Mary is received back at court by Jane and Henry, where he publicly reconciles with her, thanks to Jane's influence. After this, Mary is often at court and becomes close again with Jane, and is accorded precedence as the most important lady in court after her. Jane also tries to get Henry to reinstate Mary to the line of succession, but Henry refuses. This would not happen until his sixth wife, Katherine Parr, would take up the cause in the 1540s.

1536 - 1540: The dissolution of the monasteries - as the English church was no longer under the Pope, Henry ordered the closing of all Catholic monasteries in England and their revenue was brought to the crown.

October - March 1537: Pilgrimage of Grace - a rebellion led by the lawyer Robert Aske comprising the northern counties of England that were largely Catholic leaning, and demanded a restoration of the monasteries and the revocation of the 10 Articles. It was brutally put down by Henry's troops, and Aske and its leaders were publicly executed.

October 12th, 1537: After 3 days of labor, Jane delivers a son - Edward - the future Edward VI. Church bells are rung and bonfires lit in London to celebrate.



October 24th, 1537: Jane dies of an infection acquired during childbirth and is buried on November 12th in Winsdor Castle. Henry goes in seclusion mourning her between her death and her funeral, and puts his court into official mourning for her until February 1538.
