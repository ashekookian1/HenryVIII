Henry FitzRoy: 1519 - 1536





Childhood and Early Life: 1519 - 1533

Birth: June 15th, 1519 at Augustinian priory of St Laurence at Blackmore near Ingatestone, in Essex, England. His mother Henry's mistress at the time: Bessie Blount, was taken there from court to have her child in secret, so that the birth of Henry VIII's illegitimate son would not cause a sensation at court. (FitzRoy was illegitimate as his parents weren't married. This would thus make him ineligible to serve as his father's successor and one day possibly inherit the English throne.)

Mother: Elizabeth "Bessie" Blount, one of Henry's mistresses when he was married to Katherine of Aragon. Though after FitzRoy is born, Henry doesn't resume his affair with Bessie.

Significance of the Name: He is given the last name FitzRoy as it is medieval French for son of the king. Henry VIII used the name to emphasize to the fact that he finally had a longed for son. (Unfortunately, as England at the time had male preference primogeniture for the royal succession, sons were preferred.) Also, the first name of Henry was to honor his father: Henry VIII.

Childhood: What happened in his early childhood isn't known, but it can be speculated that he either traveled with his father's court, or was given his own living arrangements and staff to care for him.

June 18th, 1525: As Henry VIII still didn't have any legitimate sons, Henry FitzRoy is now made Duke of Richmond and Somerset and given lesser noble titles. After this, FitzRoy is now sent to Sheriff Hutton Castle (now in ruins) in Yorkshire with his own household. There he is given cleaning staff, personal staff, cooks and tutors. This would be in the same fashion that an English prince or princess at the time would be raised. Thus Henry VIII was trying to say that his son should be treated like a prince, as his father was king, and he was the old son the Henry had, so he deserved all the honors possible, legitimate or illegitimate.





Marriage and Death: 1533 - 1536

November 28th, 1533: At 14, Henry VIII arranged for FitzRoy to be married to the 14-year-old Lady Mary Howard (the accepted marriage age was lower at the time). She was the first cousin of both Anne Boleyn and Catherine Howard and from a prominent noble family. Henry VIII was here, again, trying to emphasize the grandeur and status of his son by seeking a high-ranking wife from a well known family for him.



June 8th, 1536: Second Act of Succession - passed by Parliament at Henry VIII's request saying that Henry's children with Anne Boleyn and Katherine of Aragon were illegitimate, and any heirs to the throne would come from children he had with Jane Seymour. Barring that, he would have the ability to name his successor, whether legitimate or not. It was thought that this last provision was made in case Jane Seymour (Henry's current wife) didn't give him any sons, so that way Henry VIII could designate Henry FitzRoy as his successor. Though as FitzRoy had fallen ill with TB at this time, which was believed to be soon terminal, having FitzRoy named as the sucessor wasn't taken seriously by the English people.

July 23rd, 1536: Henry FitzRoy dies, aged 17, of TB. He is buried at Thetford Priory. In 1540, when the dissolution of the monasteries was ongoing, and the priory was set to be destroyed, his grave was moved to the Church of St Michael the Archangel in Framlingham. When Mary Howard died in 1557, she was buried next to him.
