People He Had Killed

As much as he is a well-known historical personage, Henry VIII could be a rather ruthless and tyrannical ruler when it suited him. Throughout his reign, he is said to have executed people that were either: politically opposed to him, religiously opposed to him, were a threat to his throne, or that he just plain didn't like. These categories are neatly arranged below for easier reading. Please note, this is just a list of the most well-known people that Henry had killed, and there are many more people who are not listed here, as it would be, quite unfortunately, a list of hundreds.


Wives and the People Associated With Them:


Anne Boleyn - Wife #2: Executed May 19th, 1536 on false charges of adultery with five men. (First cousin of Catherine Howard - wife #5.)



George Boleyn - Anne's Younger Brother: Executed May 17th, 1536 on false charges of adultery with his sister Anne. (First cousin of Catherine Howard - wife #5.)

Mark Smeaton - Musician at the English Court: Executed May 17th, 1536 on false charges of adultery with Anne Boleyn.

Henry Norris - English Courtier: Executed May 17th, 1536 on false charges of adultery with Anne Boleyn.

William Brereton - English Courtier: Executed May 17th, 1536 on false charges of adultery with Anne Boleyn.

Francis Weston - English Courtier: Executed May 17th, 1536 on false charges of adultery with Anne Boleyn.

Catherine Howard - Wife #5: Executed February 13th, 1542 on charges of adultery with Thomas Culpeper. (First cousin of Anne Boleyn - wife #2 - and her brother George.)



Jane Parker - Lady-in-waiting to Catherine and widow of George Boleyn: Executed February 13th, 1542 on charges of helping to hide Catherine Howard and Thomas Culpeper's affair from Henry VIII.

Thomas Culpeper - Valet to Henry VIII: Executed December 10th, 1541 on charges of adultery with Catherine Howard.

Francis Dereham - Former Lover of Catherine Howard: Executed December 10th, 1541 on charges of being Catherine's former lover that no one in court knew about, and using this to blackmail his way into a job in her household as her secretary.




People Who Were Religiously Opposed to Him:

When Henry was trying to divorce his first wife, Katherine of Aragon, and marry his second wife, Anne Boleyn, he ran into opposition from the Pope; England being Catholic at the time. Ultimately, he decided that he wasn't going to abide by the Pope's decision on whether or not he could end his marriage to Katherine, and took matters into his own hands, seperating the English church from Rome and creating an offical Church of England. He continued his reforms even after he was no longer married to Anne, and the church moved in a moderate direction between Catholicism and Protestantism. Henry decided he wanted to maintain his position as a moderate reformer, and took to executing people who said that he hadn't gone far enough, or wanted the clock turned back and England made Catholic again.

Thomas More - Former Prime Minister to Henry VIII: Executed on July 6th, 1535 for refusing to take the Oath of Supremacy that Henry mandated that all his subjects take, the oath saying that he was now the head of the English church, and that it had broken with Rome.



John Fisher - Bishop of Rochester: Executed on June 22nd, 1535 for refusing to take the Oath of Supremacy that Henry mandated that all his subjects take, the oath saying that he was now the head of the English church, and that it had broken with Rome.



Robert Aske - Lawyer: Executed on July 12th, 1537 for leading a rebellion against the king called the Pilgrimage of Grace that sought to stop the dissolution of the monasteries and convents, stop religious reforms and reestablish Catholicism in England.

Baron Thomas Darcy - Nobleman: Executed on June 30th, 1537 for participating in the Pilgrimage of Grace rebellion.

Sir Robert Constable - Nobleman: Executed on July 6th, 1537 for helping lead the Pilgrimage of Grace rebellion.

Anne Askew - Protestant Preacher: Executed on July 16th 1546 for heresy by being burnt at the stake for preaching radical Protestant ideas that Henry VIII didn't approve of.

John Lassells - Protestant sympathizer and staff in Henry VIII's household - involved in bringing down Catherine Howard (wife # 5) on adultery charges: Executed on July 16th 1546 for heresy by being burnt at the stake for preaching radical Protestant ideas that Henry VIII didn't approve of.




People Who Were Politically Opposed to Him:

Thomas FitzGerald, Earl of Kildare - Irish Nobleman: Executed on February 3rd, 1537 for leading a rebellion against British rule in Ireland.



Thomas Cromwell - Prime Minister to Henry VIII: Executed on July 28th, 1540 for arranging Henry's marriage to Anne of Cleves, whom Henry wasn't fond of. The marriage ended in divorce.



Henry Howard, Earl of Surrey - Nobleman and Courtier: Executed on January 19th, 1547 on charges of treason, though really, he was very loud, brash, and called attention to himself, and generally was a nuisance in court. (First cousin of Anne Boleyn (wife #2), her brother George Boleyn, and Catherine Howard (wife #5). All three of them were also executed by Henry VIII.)






Rival Claimants For the Throne:

Henry VII had gained the throne by force when he started the Tudor dynasty, and did not have a direct hereditary claim to the throne. Therefore there was a constant stream of claimants from the previous dynasties: York and Plantagenet, that he had usurped, trying to gain back what they thought was their rightful kingdom. This problem continued into the reign of Henry VIII as well.

Edmund Pole, Earl of Suffolk - Rival claimant to the throne under the Yorkist dynasty: Executed on April 30th, 1513, for treason for claiming that as a descendant of the previous York dynasty, he had a right to the throne.

Edward Stafford, Duke of Buckingham - Rival claimant to the throne under the Plantagenet dynasty: Executed on May 17th, 1521 for plotting to kill Henry VIII.



Henry Pole - Rival claimant to the throne under the Plantagenet dynasty: Executed on January 9th, 1539 for treason, as his younger brother Reginald Pole was one of the more vocal critics of Henry VIII's religious reforms. As he was in Europe, and out of reach of Henry, in revenge, Henry had his brother killed instead. Son of Margaret Pole who was also executed because of Reginald.

Margaret Pole, Countess of Salisbury - Rival claimant to the throne under the Plantagenet dynasty: Executed on May 27th, 1541 for being the mother of one of the more vocal critics of Henry VIII's religious reforms: Cardinal Reginald Pole. As he was in Europe, and out of reach of Henry, in revenge, Henry had his mother killed instead. Mother of Henry Pole who was also executed because of Reginald.
